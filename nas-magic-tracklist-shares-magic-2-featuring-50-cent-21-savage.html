Nas Magic Tracklist Shares Magic 2 Featuring 50 Cent &amp; 21 Savage

Today, Nas revealed the tracklist for his upcoming album, Magic 2, on Instagram.

The artist had teased the project on Instagram, but only this week did he disclose the title and release date. The album has generated significant popular interest.

Nas Magic Tracklist

Nas’ forthcoming album’s tracklist was unveiled on Instagram. The album, titled “Magic 2,” is set for release today, July 21.

Fans have been eagerly awaiting this 2021 sequel to “Magic,” and recent Instagram posts from Nas have been teasing the album, gradually revealing the title and release date throughout this week.

Last week, fans inquired about Nas’ new album, prompting a Reddit user known for having intimate knowledge of the artist to drop a hint.

This only fueled the anticipation for the upcoming release.

To build even more excitement, Nas released a series of teasers after he deleted his entire Instagram account last week.

Fans are now eagerly counting down the hours until “Magic 2” finally drops.

He began by posting a threatening black-and-white video of a thunderstorm. Then, he showed a vintage video of a guy unbuckling a straight jacket while upside down in the air.

Nas is clearly adopting the appearance of an old-fashioned magician for his latest album.

Recently, he revealed the album cover for “Magic 2,” featuring two separate photographs of him wearing headphones that blend together, creating a double-vision effect.

This design is reminiscent of the cover art from Nas’ debut album, “Magic.”

Furthermore, there’s news that 50 Cent will make an appearance on the new album’s track titled “Office Hours.” The anticipation for “Magic 2” keeps growing with these exciting updates.

Nas Magic Tracklist Shares ‘Magic 2’ Featuring 50 Cent & 21 Savage

In a recent interview with XXL, Fif discussed his encounter with Nas. He expressed that he always felt Nas was brighter than his songs portrayed.

As they stood face to face, Fif observed Nas’ intelligence, thinking he needed to be cautious because Nas seemed more intellectually astute than his music suggested.

The album’s tracklist includes songs like “Intro,” “Office Hours” (featuring 50 Cent), “Abracadabra,” “Motion,” “Black Magic,” “Bokeem Woodbine,” “Slow It Down,” “Earvin Magic Johnson,” “Pistols on Your Album Cover,” “What This All Really Means,” and a bonus track titled “One Mic, One Gun” featuring 21 Savage.
